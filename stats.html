2020 Charleston, SC Real Estate Statistics

Note:

Given the 2020 pandemic, many of my clients have been asking to see up to date real estate market statistics. Given this demand, we will be updating our statistics page on the 5th of each month starting on June 5th. If you would like to see the statistics of a particular neighborhood, or want to value your home, please send me an email.

The April statistics are just starting to show the impact of Covid-19 on our market. Closed sales are down by 10% from 2020. However, we also see that active listings have declined nearly 22% from 2020. This tells us that the usual Spring market may simply be delayed until after the pandemic. Generally, real estate transactions take 30-45 days to close. We expect to see the real results of the COVID impact fully reflected in the May statistics.

There are many challenges facing our city such as: the voluntary layoffs at Boeing, the involuntary layoffs at Benefit Focus and MUSC.

While the future is uncertain given the economic climate during this crisis, we do remain hopeful for a few reasons as we begin the process of reopening the city. First, we believe that this crisis will increase the frequency of remote workers. As this happens, we believe that remote workers will leave the crowded larger markets and move to areas with lower population. As you all know, Charleston is still and will always be a special place for so many people. Second, the decline in the markets along with high unemployment could trigger many to retire early. This is important to follow given the aging population of the US.

Despite this pandemic, we have still continued to close deals. We hope you and your family stay positive and stay safe during these unusual times.
